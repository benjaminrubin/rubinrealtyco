Our Philosophy

When you buy or sell real estate, especially a home, it is likely the biggest investment of your life. In going through the buying or selling process, the first step is to separate fact from fiction, and find an agent with knowledge and experience and someone you can trust.

The following are common facts and misconceptions about that process:

This, of course, is one of the most important decisions you will make if you are a seller. As a matter of contract law, when you offer your property for a particular price, you are not necessarily bound by that. Once you set the price, and it is marketed at that amount, there will be a buyer or buyers that make an offer. You can accept the offer based on the terms offered, or you are free to make a counteroffer. It is your acceptance of the offer that creates the binding agreement, not the offer to purchase from the buyer.

If you are a seller, you are hoping for the best possible price. However, it is not the seller that establishes the ultimate price, but the market. So, how does one establish an asking price? A knowledgeable broker will do a market analysis, starting with comparable sales. Here she searches for and reviews sales of properties with characteristics similar to your property in the general geographical area. In most cases, a buyer will not make an offer to purchase property that is priced substantially above what the market will bear. The best approach is to price the property in line with the market, and then let the market do its work. If you get multiple offers, that does not mean the property was underpriced. It may mean that the market is heating up, and that is just a trend. If you do not get any offers for weeks, or even months, you could safely assume that the property is overpriced.

Some agents will convince you to under price your property in order to get a quick sale, and a quick commission. That is not necessarily in the best interests of the seller. Other agents may accept a listing for any price the seller demands in order to get the listing. You can expect at that price will be above the market price, and you can expect that there will be numerous price reductions. However, during that process, you may lose well-qualified buyers that that would have made a solid offer at the market price.

In conclusion, there are many variables and it is best to rely on a broker with knowledge and experience who you can trust.

You get what you pay for. The commission charged by the agent is negotiable. The commission is split between the buyer and seller agent, and also between the brokerage house and a particular agent. The standard commission is 6%, though that can be reduced. If substantially reduced, you will not have an agent that is properly motivated to do his or her best job for you. Again, you are dealing with an asset of enormous value. You must be careful, and like most things, there is a balance that must be achieved.

There is no perfect answer to this question. Each property is different, each property is unique. There is a range of property condition from tear down to turnkey. The tear down property is sold for land value only. Turnkey means you turn the key, and do nothing else but move in. Your property could be any place in between those extremes. Many buyers are looking for a fixer with the idea that they will spend their own money to make repairs and do remodeling. The price you ask will have to reflect that. Other buyers are looking for a turnkey property, with no work to be done at all. (Sometimes properties are sold with their furnishings).

The problem with remodeling to achieve a turnkey condition is that you will be spending substantial amounts to remodel. You may never recover the amounts you invest in remodeling. Also, the finished product may not be to the taste of a prospective buyer. That applies to everything from remodeling kitchens, bathrooms, to the color of a particular tile and the design of the faucets. Spending money to remodel property, you must avoid any actions that restrict the nature and type of buyers that will be interested in purchasing your property. Again, each property is different, and that is another reason why you must work with a Woodland Hills real estate broker with knowledge and experience.

The number of sales, even if true, is not a true indicator of the competency of an agent. Firstly, the claim may not be true or verifiable. Even if it is, it could mean that that particular agent has a bigger budget for self-promotion, or takes listings at over market prices in order to get the listing in the first place. A larger sales volume may also mean that the particular agent will have less time to spend on your listing. This is true even if that agent claims that they are part of a “team” (see below).

Not true. In large brokerage firms, agents come and go. The entry-level agents, or those with little experience, are usually supervised by agents with more experience. There are key, fundamental methods of marketing properties for sale. The large brokers do not have any monopoly on those methods. A large brokerage firm may have a large advertising budget, but it does not mean that your property will sell any faster or at a higher price. Larger firms may not be as efficient as smaller firms. Also, what is the true advantage of an agent working with a “team?” Does that mean that he or she lacks the confidence to work alone? How are the duties and responsibilities of marketing and selling your property divided, and what is the purpose of that division of labor? There is no harm in asking questions. In my opinion, you are better off with a smaller, better focused office, such as mine.

There are no set rules in any aspect of selling. If you are satisfied with the price and terms of the first offer, then it may be the best offer. The first offer may come from a buyer who has already visited many properties, and is confident that yours is the one he or she is seeking. Remember, the offer alone is only the start. Your agent must be able to evaluate the terms of each offer, as well as the financial ability of the buyer. That particular set of skills will hopefully avoid you wasting time in an escrow that will never close. What if you get an all cash offer? This means that you do not have to wait until a condition of financing is satisfied. That means the buyer does not have to go looking for a loan to buy your property, a process that is often time-consuming. However, each offer must stand on its own and your real estate agent must discuss all aspects of an offer, including terms and conditions.
